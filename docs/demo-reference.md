# Lush Qatar Online Store: Migration to Shopify
## Demo and Training Session

Thank you for making the time to meet. This document outlines what we will cover today so you can follow along and refer back to it afterward. The goal is to show you how your new Shopify store works day to day, walk through your own products and data already loaded into a private preview store, and agree on the few practical steps needed to move forward. Time is reserved at the end for your questions.

### At a glance

- Format: a guided walkthrough of the Shopify admin, followed by questions.
- Duration: about 45 minutes, with 10 to 15 minutes for questions.
- Preview store: a private Shopify store preloaded with a sample of your live catalogue.
- Reference: your new store will mirror the look and feel of the Lush Saudi Arabia store (lush.sa.com).

### 1. Where the project stands

- Access to your current WooCommerce store is in place, and we have completed a full review of your existing data.
- Your current store holds around 538 products, 61 categories, roughly 1,950 registered customers, and more than 3,100 orders. All of this is included in the migration.
- A private Shopify preview store has been prepared and preloaded with a representative sample of your real products, collections, customers, and orders, so today's walkthrough reflects your actual business rather than placeholder content.

### 2. The walkthrough

We will move through the parts of the store you and your team will use most often.

- Products: how a product looks in Shopify, images and galleries, pricing, and variants such as sizes, and how you edit or add one.
- Collections: how products are grouped, for example Bath and Shower, Bath Bombs, and Best Sellers, to organize the storefront.
- Orders: how an order appears, its status, and how you process it.
- Customers: customer profiles and their history.
- Reports and analytics: sales and performance at a glance.
- Navigation and storefront: menus, search, and how the store presents to shoppers, using the Saudi store as the visual reference.

### 3. What is already loaded

To keep the session relevant, we loaded a live sample from your store:

- A selection of your best selling products, with images.
- Their collections.
- A set of customers.
- Recent orders.

This sample is for demonstration only and will be cleared before the full migration, which brings across your complete catalogue, customers, order history, content, and search rankings.

### 4. Managing the store yourselves

A core part of our approach is your independence. You will have full, direct access to your store dashboard, and this session doubles as training so your team can manage products, collections, orders, and content without depending on an agency. A recorded training session is included.

### 5. Practical steps to move forward

To keep the timeline on track, a few items are best actioned together:

- Store access: adding the right members of your team, with appropriate roles, including access for the brand manager so plan selection and billing can be completed.
- Theme and plan: choosing the Shopify plan and purchasing the Be Yours theme, both billed directly to you through Shopify.
- Payments: setting up a Qatar payment gateway, as Shopify's own payment product is not available in Qatar.
- What we will need from you: your desired features and any custom requirements, and coordination with your IT team for the domain switch when we are ready to launch.

### 6. Indicative timeline

- Store setup and theme configuration.
- Full data migration and internal testing.
- Your review and approval.
- Training and launch, with a zero downtime domain switch and monitoring in the days that follow.

### 7. Questions

We have set aside time at the end for any questions from the brand and IT teams. Please feel free to raise anything specific you would like to see or understand in more detail.
